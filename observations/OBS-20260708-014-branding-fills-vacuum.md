# Brand invention appears when content or style dimensions are unspecified

## Fact

P-NO-CONTENT's artifact was self-named "Sprout" (logo mark, sidebar brand, file named Sprout.dc.html), greeted a named persona ("Good morning, Alex" with an editable name in Settings), and carried a tagline ("small steps, counted kindly"). P-NO-STYLE's artifact displayed the brand "HABITLY" in its status bar. P-FULL and P-NO-AUDIENCE artifacts, generated from prompts with more dimensions specified, showed no invented product name.

## Interpretation

When the brief leaves identity-adjacent dimensions open, the agent fills the vacuum with a coherent invented brand (name, voice, persona) rather than leaving the artifact generic — consistent with treating a prototype as a believable product simulation rather than a wireframe. Alternative: naming may correlate with layout scale (desktop sidebar affords a logo slot) rather than specification gaps.

## Confidence and limitations

Pattern over five runs in one domain; the correlation has plausible confounds (layout, run variance).

## Follow-up

Ask an identical brief with an explicit product name and check whether invented branding disappears.
