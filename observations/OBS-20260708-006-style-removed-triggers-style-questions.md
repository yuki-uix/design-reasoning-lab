# Removing style triggered heavy style questioning, including visual options

## Fact

In P-NO-STYLE (visual style and tone sentences removed), at least four of eight intake questions were style-related: overall vibe (four mood options), color direction (four rendered swatch thumbnails), typography feel, and copywriting tone. The vibe question's subtitle echoed the retained audience ("For busy working parents, 30-45"). P-VAGUE likewise asked vibe and showed color swatches. Streak-visualization questions in other runs also used rendered thumbnails.

## Interpretation

Style is on the elicit side of the boundary, contradicting the pre-registered expectation that style would be silently defaulted from strong priors. Combined with OBS-20260708-004 and -005, the observed boundary is: audience assumed; content and style asked. Visual answer options (swatches, thumbnails) show elicitation is multimodal, not text-only.
